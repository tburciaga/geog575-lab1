The Growth of a Nation

Introduction

The United States Census Bureau's (USCB) first statistical atlas was produced in 1874 to mark the nation's upcoming centennial and to show the progress of the nation over the previous century. The atlas represented the country's first use of comparative statistics at a national level and provided a means of quickly interpreting complex demographic phenomena at a national level.

USCB produced statistical atlases for the next several decades, but by the turn of the 20th century, interest in producing a national atlas had declined, and despite monumental shifts in the nation's population in the following decades, no National Statistical atlas was produced between 1930 and 2000.1

Ranking the Population of Urban Areas

The following map uses data compiled by USCB to analyze the growth of cities in the coterminous 48 states between 1790 and 20202/4. Instead of representing urban populations using total counts, the map normalizes city population changes against national population changes by using the relative population rank to track the rise in westward migration.

How to Use the Map

Controls

The map has several controls that facilitate analysis of the data3. In the top-left and top-right corners of the map window are controls for zooming the map window to examine the data at different scales as well as a layer control for switching the basemap and activating the city rank overlay layer. In the bottom-right corner of the map window is the sequence control, which allows for cycling through 230 years of census records to visualize the city ranking data.

Analysis

The legend in the bottom-left corner shows a readout of the year being displayed as well as the city rank symbol legend. To view the name and rank of a city shown on the map, hover over the city's circle symbol to activate a popup. The city will be highlighted in yellow.
